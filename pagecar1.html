Chevrolet Camaro ZL1 1LE

History

The Camaro began its life back in 1966 when Chevrolet, one of the largest American automotive companies, decided to make a competitor to Ford's Mustang. The term Pony Car is being used to classify this vehicle, and the big engine, the 2+2 seating and the famous coupe look with massive wheels are the main characteristics on these cars. The Camaro name was reportedly derived from French word “camarade”, and it means “friend”, but Chevy told the press that a Camaro is a small, vicious animal that eats Mustangs. This Pony stayed in production from 1966 up until 2002. Chevrolet offered the last model to celebrate the 35th anniversary in 2002, and it came with some pretty special features. In 2009 we got a glimpse at a concept car and in 2010 its was revealed as the 5th generation of the Chevrolet Camaro. The Pony is still going strong with the 6th generation available since 2015


Engine

The present-day Camaro comes with multiple powertrain options. The 2.0L inline-4 Eco-Boost which pushes 275 hp, A newer 3.6L V6 that does 335 hp and a 6.5L V8 with 455 horses. The ZL1 model which adds a supercharger for some forced induction to push out a massive 650 horsepower. The top trim can do the 0-60 miles in around 3.9 seconds and the quarter-mile in 12.4 seconds with a top speed of 325km/h.


Aesthetic

Camaro’s looks have changed over the years, it went from the original American muscle car look to today’s a never, streamlined, futuristic and aggressive design. The design makes the car lighter, more aerodynamic and the overall aesthetic is gorgeous.
