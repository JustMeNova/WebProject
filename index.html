About

American Muscle

The debut of the American muscle car first began in 1949 with the Oldsmobile Rocket 88. This car had a V8 engine and produced a total of 165 horsepower. This amount of horsepower is nowhere near todays standard of American muscle Cars. Today's muscle cars can easily surpass 300 horsepower on the cheaper base models and reach the 1000 horsepower mark on top of the line models like the Mustang Shelby GT500, The Chevrolet Camaro ZL1 and the Dodge Challanger SRT Demon.


Japanese Sport

Japanese sport cars are better known as Japanese Domestic Market vehicles (J.D.M.). These cars are made to conform to Japan's strict vehicle rules. A lot of sought-after Japanese sports cars get imported to the U.S. and Canada from auctions. The Datsun S211 was the first Japanese roadster made by Datsun, now knows as Nissan. The car's legacy has been succeeded by the Nissan Z-line, which is still very popular among fans.


European Luxury

The European luxury car market is big, and teeming with history. From Karl Benz's creation of the first internal combustion engine inside a car, BMW's inline six engines to newer innovations like audi's Quattro system, its no wonder these cars are so popular today. European cars come with higher comfort, sleek yet powerful designs, incredible performance and unrivaled technology. The production quality standard is very high, and that is reflected in the car prices. Audi, BMW and Mercedes are prime examples of European luxury car brands that constantly push the lines of how we enjoy driving today.
