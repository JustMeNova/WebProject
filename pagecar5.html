Subaru WRX STI

History

In 1988 Subaru launched its performance motorsport line called the Subaru Tecnica International which is now known as the STI. The first time it was entered in a competition, the 220 hp car broke an FIA World Speed Endurance Record. It Averaged 222 km/h over 99, 779km in 20 days. This car would later win three championships (1995, 1996, 1997). The Subaru Impreza WRX STI would accumulate a total of 46 rally wins. But in 2008 the Subaru Impreza WRX STI was withdrawn from the World Rally Championship due to The Great Recession. In 2015 the Subaru Impreza WRX STI was discontinued to create its own path as the Subaru WRX STI.


Engine

Subaru is known for their reliability and that’s because they’ve stayed with the same engine from the beginning of the Subaru Impreza WRX STI. As the years went by Subaru would slightly tweak their boxer engine. This engine is in every car but there are two versions to it. For SUV the boxer engine came with 6 cylinders. The STI comes with a 4-cylinder engine and that for a good reason. This would allow the car to be lighter. The 2.5L turbocharged engine produces a total of 310 horsepower at 6,000 rpm and 290lbs of torque.


Tuning

This car is known all over the car communities. This car has been pushed to its limits stripped and re-constructed. The most popular modifications done to this car are bigger turbos to increase the amount of air to be compressed and increase the cars speed. The STI is usually stripped to decrease its weight and increaser the cars respective. This also allows to car to gain speed at a quicker rate. This STI comes with a stock spoiler but is usually replaced by a carbon fiber spoiler to decrease its weight and gives the car a sportier look.
