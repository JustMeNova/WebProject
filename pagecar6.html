Mazda RX-7

History

The first-generation Mazda RX-7 was first released in 1978. The first generation of the RX-7 it was built from 1978-1984. The RX-7 was a 1.2L rotary engine produced a total of 100 horsepower and 105 lbs of torque. It 0-60 time was less than 10 seconds which was acceptable during that time. The car reached a maximum rpm of 7,000. This vehicle was known for its super light-weight coupe body. Over the years the car would receive multiple new rotary engines until its final generation. The most loved Mazda RX-7 was produced from 1992 up until 2002. This car was loved due to its cheap price of $42 000 and the fact that it could compete with expensive sport cars of the time such as the Porsche 911. This 2-door front-engine, rear wheel drive vehicle was on its way to become one of the greatest JDM vehicles of all time.


Engine

The third generation RX-7 engine was among one of the greatest engines of its time. The 13B-REW engine was a 1.3 L twin-turbo charged, four port rotary engine. The engine generates da total of 255 horsepower and 217 lbs of torque at 5,000 rpm. The car weighed a total of 2,800 pounds. The cars transmission came with a 4-speed automatic or a 5-speed manual. This car would perform best as a 5-speed manual.


Tuning

The most popular modifications on this car are the cat back, a downpipe, intake and a boost controller. This helps the engine reach the ideal car person goal to reach 1,000 horsepower. A modified cat back allows the car to gain 15 horsepower and a great sounding engine. An aftermarket downpipe removes a good amount of heat from the engine bay and allows the engine to gain more horsepower. By changing the Intake, the cars air filtration is improved by 10 times which also allows the engine to gain more horsepower. The boost controller is the most important tuning piece you can get for your turbos. This lets you know how your turbos are doing and how hard you can push them.
