Toyota Supra MK 4

History

The first Toyota Supra appeared in 1978 and the production of this model ended three years later in 1981. The original name of this car was the Toyota Celica Supra, which then split into 2 different cars, such as the Toyota Celica and the Toyota Supra. It originally came with a 2.0L, 6-cylinder engine that produced a total of 123hp. It started of as a three-door hot hatchback vehicle but later evolved into a two door coupe. The Toyota Supra that everyone loves and dreams of is the fourth generation. The fourth generation was produced from 1993-2002. The Supra came in four different models but there was one that stood out from the others.


Engine

The fourth generation Toyota Supra was a 3.0L 6-cylinder engine which produced a total of 330hp. This rear wheel car had an impressive 0-60MPH of 6.4 seconds. The top speed of this vehicle from the factory is 250 km/h. The six gear transition box produced a torque of 325lb-ft at 4800 RPM.


Tuning

The name 2JZ would be known by all car communities. The 2JZ engine is so popular due to its high tuning abilities. With the 2JZ there are no limits. This engine has gained such a demand that this engine has doubled in price. According to Fastcar, “a full exhaust system and induction kit will free up some more power. But for big power gains a large single turbo conversion is usually the first port of call, and with some people even managing 1000bhp on standard internals, you can see the potential is there to achieve pretty much anything you want.”.
