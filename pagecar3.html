Dodge Challenger SRT Hellcat

History

The Challenger was introduced back in 1970 and its was pretty much a rival to Ford’s Mustang and Chevy’s Camaro. The Challenger only had 3 generations since its beginning and only of those were pony cars. From 1970-1974 we had the first gen Challenger coming in a hardtop and convertible body styles. From 1978-1983 Dodge rebadge a Mitsubishi Galant Lambda under their own brand and imported the car to the U.S. to sell under the Challenger title. Being an economical car hiding under a pony name, it didn’t get much popularity. After taking a long break, Dodge came back in 2008 and released the 3rd generation of the Challenger. It was a direct answer to the Ford mustang and the Camaro just like in the old days. The Dodge Challenger Demon used to be the most powerful pony out of them all and was a heavily modified SRT Hellcat, but it got discontinued and the SRT Hellcat took its place


Engine

The Challenger comes with a lot of options to chose from, the SXT trim level coming with a 3.6L V6 305 hp engine, the R/T trim has a 5.7L V8 making 375 hp, the Scat Pack has a 6.4L V8 making 485 hp and the SRT Hellcat trim, coming in at $90,000 has a massive 6.2 Hellcat V8 with a supercharger strapped on top of it making an astounding 797 horses and 707 lb-ft of torque at 4500 rpm. The 0-60 on the Hellcat is 3.7 seconds, it can run the quarter-mile in 11.8 seconds and its top speed is locked at 325km/h.


Aesthetic

The Hellcat looks very similar to its 1970 counterpart. Dodge managed to modernize the American muscle while keeping its rough look. The two eye looking headlights with a front grille that looks like a mouth gives it the aggressive look we like in American cars. Sometimes they come outfitted with massive hood scoops.
