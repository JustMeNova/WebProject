Ford Mustang Shelby GT500

History

The Ford Mustang is a pony that has been in continuous productions since 1964. It is the longest produced car made by Ford and it has reached the sixth generation. This pony car was initially distinguished by its long hood and short deck, giving it a somewhat sporty and aggressive look. The mustang shook the world and led to multiple competitors to start developing cars around the world. At first the mustang seemed more like a car for women, but even with that over 400,000 units were sold in the first year. Carol Shelby, a young man who liked to modify cars asked for a deal with ford and created the Shelby Cobra, later on he went on to design the Mustang Shelby GT500, which is still the highest trim model for the Ford Mustang among other special models like the GT, Mach 1, Boss 302/429 and the Bullit.


Engine

The new 6th generation Mustang comes with some new powertrain options for its different trims and price points. The 4-cylinder 2.3L Eco-Boost allows the car to push the same 310 horsepower of a V6 engine for a cheaper price, the now discontinued 3.7L V6 that did 300 hp and the legendary 5.0L Coyote V8 that does 435 hp. In 2020 the re-introductions of the GT500 includes a 5.2L Predator V8 with a 2.65L supercharger to push more air into the engine to produce a massive 760 horsepower and 625lb-ft of tourque at 5000 rpm. The GT500 manages a 0-60 miles in 3.4 seconds a quarter-mile in 11.3 seconds and has a top speed of 290km/h.


Aesthetic

The Mustang Shelby GT500 is a unique car, it brought the 5th gen look and modernized it to be more aerodynamic, aggressive, and just overall looking like a beast. The 6th gen is undeniably the one of the most visible sports cars on the road. It features headlights that look like eyes and some sequential taillights that just stand out, even though they manage to look like the ones on the original 1964 mustang.
