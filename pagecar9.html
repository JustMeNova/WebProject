Mercedes Benz-AMG GTR Coupé

History

"The Mercedes-AMG GT is a grand tourer produced in coupé and roadster bodystyles by German automobile manufacturer Mercedes-AMG" It was introduced in 2014, and unveiled at the Paris Motor Show. It later went on sale in 2015 in two variants, the GT and the GTS. In 2016, a high performance variant was introduced, and that is the AMG GTR that we know today. Upon launch though similar to the Mercedes came with some more sporty aesthetic changes like the front grille, the back wing as well as new front intakes, and front and rear defusers. The model was comparable to the GT3 which was a racing variant of the AMG GT. Though the car may not be a purely racing adaptation of the GT, it cleared a lap on the Nürburgring Nordschleife in just 7:10.92 minutes, making it the 5th fastest lap time for a road legal vehicle at the time.


Engine

This high-performance variant of Mercedes' AMG GT sports a handcrafted 7-speed 4.0 litre AMG V8 biturbo engine that can produce up to 577 horsepower and 516 lbs of torque. The 4-wheel drive Mercedes GTR claims a strong speed of 317 kmph with a 0-100 time of 3.6 seconds.


Luxury

Though The Mercedes Benz AMG GTR is most famed for its sporty look and track pace the car's luxury does not end with the engine nor exterior. Mercedes has kept a clean minimalistic interior but has spared no expense on comfort. The seats are bolstered and covered with exclusive Nappa Leather DINAMICA upholstery and roofliner. The center console is equipped with few buttons and a rotary shifter allowing for a clean look. The car's custimizeability lies with it's HD heads-up display and strong Burmester Surroun System so that driving may be comfortable and enjoyable.
