Audi RS7

History and Quattro System

The Audi RS7 was originally created as a high powered sport version of Audi's S7 and A7 sportback. The Audi RS7 debuted in 2014 and was recognized as Audi's most powerful four door production vehicle ever introduced. The 2014 Audi RS7 is equipped with a twin-turbocharged, 4.0-liter V-8 engine that could deliver 560 horsepower and 553 lbs of torque and is equipped with Cylinder Demand technology which allows it to operate using only 4 of its 8 cylinders on the highway. In 1980 audi had unveiled it's new quattro system and tested it in the Ur-Quattro. "The quattro all-wheel drive system quickly became an icon for its lightweight design, superior traction, handling and control."

Audi Website


Engine

The current 2021 model of Audi's RS7 is slightly improved compared to its 2014 predecessor. The 4.0 liter V8 boasts a top speed of 305 kmph as well as a 0-100 time of 3.9 seconds. The car can produce 591 horsepower and 590 lbs of torque and is equipped with Audi's famous quattro system.
